## About This Project

This repository contains a Portuguese translation and adaptation of the content from [goodtheorist.science](https://www.goodtheorist.science/), a resource created by Nobel Prize-winning physicist Gerard 't Hooft.

**[Access the Complete Content Index](indice.md)**

## The Original Project

The site "How to become a GOOD Theoretical Physicist" was created by Gerard 't Hooft as a resource for students and enthusiasts who wish to learn theoretical physics through self-study. The goal is to provide a structured study roadmap covering everything from mathematical foundations to the most advanced topics in modern theoretical physics.

Gerard 't Hooft is a Dutch physicist who received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1999 for his contributions to the quantum theory of fields. He created this website to share his knowledge and guide those who wish to follow the path of theoretical physics, even without access to a formal university education.

For more information about the author, see [About the Author](sobre-o-autor.md).

## Project Philosophy

In the author's own words:

> "This is a site for young students - and anyone else - who are (like me) fascinated by the challenges posed by real science, and who are - like me - determined to use their brains to discover new things about the physical world we live in. In short, it is for everyone who has decided to study theoretical physics in their own time."

The author compares Theoretical Physics to a skyscraper:

> "Theoretical Physics is like a skyscraper. It has solid foundations in elementary mathematics and notions of classical physics (pre-20th century). Don't think that pre-20th century physics is 'irrelevant' just because we have so much more now. In those days, the solid foundations of the knowledge we enjoy now were established. Don't try to build your skyscraper without first reconstructing these foundations yourself."

## Recommended Study Approach

The author emphasizes the importance of:

1.  **Studying Seriously**: "Don't complain that it seems like a lot. You won't get your Nobel Prize for free, and remember, all of this together takes our students at least 5 years of intense study."
2.  **Doing Exercises**: "It is necessary to do exercises. Some of the texts come with exercises. Do them, or better, invent your own exercises."
3.  **Questioning and Verifying**: "You don't need to believe anything you read out of faith - check. Try alternative approaches, as many as you can."
4.  **Being Persistent**: "Even if you are extremely intelligent, it is still likely that you will get stuck somewhere. Navigate the internet yourself. Find more."

## Goal of This Repository

This repository aims to translate and adapt the original website's content into Brazilian Portuguese, maintaining the original structure and philosophy. The objective is to make these valuable resources accessible to a wider Portuguese-speaking audience.
